About Body Mass Index (BMI)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation that uses your height and weight to determine if you're in a healthy weight range. It's calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in meters squared (kg/m²).

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it has limitations. It doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat mass, so athletes or very muscular individuals may have a high BMI despite being healthy. Similarly, it may not accurately reflect health status in elderly individuals or certain ethnic groups.

Important: BMI is just one indicator of health. Always consult with healthcare professionals for comprehensive health assessment and personalized advice.

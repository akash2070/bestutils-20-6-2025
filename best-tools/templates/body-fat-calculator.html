About Body Fat Calculator

Our free body fat calculator uses the U.S. Navy Body Fat Formula to estimate your body fat percentage based on circumference measurements. This method provides a practical alternative to expensive body composition testing and is widely used by military and fitness professionals.

Body fat percentage is a more accurate indicator of health and fitness than BMI alone, as it distinguishes between muscle mass and fat mass, providing better insights for fitness goals and health assessment.

How to Use the Body Fat Calculator

  1. Select Gender: Choose male or female (affects required measurements)
  2. Choose Units: Select imperial (inches) or metric (centimeters)
  3. Measure Height: Enter your height in the selected unit
  4. Measure Neck: Measure neck circumference at the narrowest point
  5. Measure Waist: Measure waist at the narrowest point (males) or at navel level (females)
  6. Measure Hips (Females Only): Measure hips at the widest point
  7. Calculate: Click "Calculate Body Fat" to get your estimated percentage

Body Fat Measurement Tips

  • Consistent Timing: Measure at the same time of day for consistency
  • Proper Technique: Keep measuring tape snug but not tight against skin
  • Relaxed Position: Stand naturally with normal breathing
  • Multiple Measurements: Take 2-3 measurements and use the average
  • Track Progress: Monitor changes over time rather than single measurements
  • Healthy Ranges: Men: 10-20%, Women: 16-25% for fitness levels
  • Professional Comparison: Compare with DEXA or hydrostatic weighing when possible

Understanding Body Fat Percentage

Essential Fat: Men: 2-5%, Women: 10-13% - minimum needed for basic physiological functions.

Athletic Range: Men: 6-13%, Women: 14-20% - typical for competitive athletes.

Fitness Range: Men: 14-17%, Women: 21-24% - good fitness level for general population.

Accuracy Note: Navy formula provides estimates within ±3-4% of more precise methods like DEXA scans.

About Ohm's Law Calculator

Ohm's Law is a fundamental principle in electrical engineering that describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit. Our calculator makes it easy to find any unknown value when you know the other two.

This tool is essential for electronics students, electrical engineers, technicians, and hobbyists working with circuits. It can calculate voltage (V), current (I), resistance (R), and power (P) using the standard Ohm's Law formulas.

The calculator also includes power calculations, making it a comprehensive tool for electrical circuit analysis and design.

Example Calculations

Example 1: Find Current

Given: Voltage = 12V, Resistance = 4Ω

I = V ÷ R = 12V ÷ 4Ω = 3A

P = V × I = 12V × 3A = 36W

Example 2: Find Resistance

Given: Voltage = 24V, Current = 2A

R = V ÷ I = 24V ÷ 2A = 12Ω

P = V × I = 24V × 2A = 48W

Example 3: Find Voltage

Given: Current = 0.5A, Resistance = 100Ω

V = I × R = 0.5A × 100Ω = 50V

P = I² × R = 0.5² × 100 = 25W

Example 4: Find Power

Given: Voltage = 9V, Current = 1.5A

P = V × I = 9V × 1.5A = 13.5W

R = V ÷ I = 9V ÷ 1.5A = 6Ω

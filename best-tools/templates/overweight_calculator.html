What is BMI and How Does it Determine Overweight?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used screening tool that calculates your body fat based on your height and weight. It helps determine whether you're underweight, at a normal weight, overweight, or obese, which can indicate potential health risks.

BMI Formula

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)²
or
BMI = (weight (lbs) ÷ height (inches)²) × 703

Understanding Your BMI Categories

Underweight (BMI < 18.5)

May indicate malnutrition, eating disorders, or other health issues. Consider consulting a healthcare provider.

Normal Weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)

Indicates a healthy weight range with lower risk of chronic diseases.

Overweight (BMI 25.0-29.9)

May increase risk of heart disease, diabetes, and other health conditions.

Obese (BMI ≥ 30.0)

Significantly increases risk of serious health problems including cardiovascular disease.

Limitations of BMI

  • • Doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat: Athletes may have high BMI due to muscle mass
  • • Age and gender variations: Body composition changes with age and differs between genders
  • • Ethnicity differences: Different ethnic groups may have varying health risks at the same BMI
  • • Body fat distribution: Doesn't account for where fat is stored (belly vs. hips)

⚠️ Important Health Disclaimer:

  • • BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic tool
  • • Consult healthcare professionals for comprehensive health assessment
  • • Consider other factors like waist circumference, muscle mass, and overall health
  • • This calculator is for adults aged 18 and older
  • • Results may not be accurate for pregnant women, athletes, or elderly individuals

🎯 Tips for Healthy Weight Management:

  • • Focus on balanced nutrition with whole foods
  • • Include regular physical activity (150+ minutes per week)
  • • Stay hydrated and get adequate sleep
  • • Set realistic, sustainable weight goals
  • • Monitor progress through multiple metrics, not just weight

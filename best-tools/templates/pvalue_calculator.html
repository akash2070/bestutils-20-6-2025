🔬 Statistical Tests Explained

📊 T-Test (One Sample)

Tests whether a sample mean differs significantly from a known population mean. Used when population standard deviation is unknown.

Use when: Comparing sample mean to known value, small sample size (n < 30)

📈 T-Test (Two Sample)

Compares the means of two independent samples to determine if they differ significantly.

Use when: Comparing two group means, independent samples

🎯 Z-Test

Tests hypotheses about population means when the population standard deviation is known and sample size is large.

Use when: Large sample size (n ≥ 30), known population standard deviation

🔄 Chi-Square Test

Tests the association between categorical variables or goodness of fit to expected distributions.

Use when: Categorical data, testing independence or goodness of fit

🎓 Understanding P-Values

A p-value is the probability of obtaining test results at least as extreme as the observed results, assuming the null hypothesis is true. It's a crucial concept in statistical hypothesis testing.

🔴

p < 0.05

Statistically significant - reject null hypothesis

🟡

0.05 ≤ p < 0.10

Marginally significant - consider context

🟢

p ≥ 0.10

Not significant - fail to reject null hypothesis

📌 Key Points to Remember

  • • A low p-value (< 0.05) suggests strong evidence against the null hypothesis
  • • P-values don't indicate the size or importance of an effect
  • • Always consider the context and practical significance of results
  • • P-values should be interpreted alongside confidence intervals
